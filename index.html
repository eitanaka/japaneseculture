About Japan

Japanese Culture

Japan is an island nation surrounded by the sea and rich in nature.

As such an island nation, Japan's culture is characterized by the idea of not going against nature and respecting the flow of nature in everything.

Religion

The Japanese religion is Buddhism, which originated in ancient India and was introduced to Japan via China and Korea.

However, Buddhism was added to the original Japanese thought and changed into a religion that "feels" the power of God.

Arhitecture

In Japanese architecture, wood is mainly used, and technology makes lightweight materials strong.

Because Japan is particularly prone to natural disasters such as earthquakes and typhoons, lightweight and sturdy structures have been built.

Customs

As Orientals, Japanese people tend to value harmony and excel at cooperative thinking.

Japan is prone to earthquakes and tsunamis, and there is a tendency to coexist with natural threats, which is the essence of Japanese culture.

Explore by Interest

Food

Japan is a foodie's paradise, with some of the finest and most varied cuisine in the world.

Sports

Japanese culture is intertwined with sport, whether it be kendo and kyudo, originally developed for hunting and combat, judo, a combat sport with the philosophical aim of perfecting one's character or the must see cultural sport experience, sumo, the ancient Shinto ritual that has represented Japan as a national sport for centuries.

Festival

Some matsuri date back almost a thousand years, and months and months of preparation and hard work go into making them a success.

Spring

Spring is generally from March to May in Japan.

“Sakura” or cherry blossoms are a symbol of spring in Japan.

Many people gather under sakura trees for “ohanami,”

which means cherry blossom viewing, all over Japan.

Summer

Summer is usually from June to August.

You can enjoy swimming in various places.

The islands of Okinawa are recommended to enjoy a relaxing time.

Fall

September to November is usually the fall season.

The most comfortable and nicest weather comes in this season.

Visit tourist attractions anywhere in Japan that are

famous for leaves changing colors and delight in the beauty.

Winter

Winter is from December to February.

It is the coldest season of the year,

which is perfect for enjoying hot springs.

The beautiful winter scenery is exceptionally remarkable.
